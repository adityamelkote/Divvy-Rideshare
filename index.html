Divvy Rideshare Analysis Trends in the City of Chicago

Rideshare vehicles is already a common method of transportation in most major cities, but Divvy has redefined the concept of ride-sharing through the introduction of compact personal vehicles. Here is the breakdown of the following trends regarding Chicago rideshare data in the month of March 2022.



Introducing the data, some basic introductary statistics are as follows:

The most popular station was: Streeter Dr & Grand Ave

The most popular day to commute was Wednesdays. Classic Bike's tend to be the most commonly used method of transportation.

The 5th of March 2022 was the day with the highest amount of traffic, which happened to fall on a Saturday.

The most popular station was: Streeter Dr & Grand Ave. However, Ellis Ave. 55th Street - 60th Street at the greatest traffic inbetween any two stations. In fact, the traffic is so high at these two stations that it suggests that this is a major hub for transportation, perhaps situated in a very highly trafficked district. The highest concentration of ride-sharing was mostly on the north-east side of Chicago, with a small pocket near Ellis Ave, perhaps suggesting that most of the ride-sharing is done downtown. Divvy's service also extends past the Chicago region, demonstrated by stations north of the city limits.







Amongst those who are members, Classic bikes tend to be the most highly used form of ride-sharing, especially on Wednesdays. Electric bikes tend to follow a similar trend, perhaps suggesting that members are mostly people who use this service to commute. Docked bikes appear to not exist as an option for members. These observations changes when looking at casual riders. While classic bikes still tend to be the dominant form of ride-sharing and there is a spike on Wednesdays still, the weekend tends to have far more riders. It is interesting to note that most users tend to be members, rather then casuals.
